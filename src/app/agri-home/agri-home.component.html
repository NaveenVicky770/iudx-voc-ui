AgriJSON

  • AgriJSON is a schema that provides a standard method of defining the semantics and for representation of agricultural data and enables a seamless exchange across the ecosystem of digital agriculture.
  • The concept of AgriJSON was developed by Mr Satyanarayana Jeedigunta, Chief Advisor, C4IR India. The version 1.0 of AgriJSON schema has been developed by a team of IISc, Bengaluru.
  • Currently, AgriJSON is developed on a 3-level taxonomy - Concepts, Categories and Types. The five concepts of AgriJSON are: Entity, naturalResource, Process, Service and Calssification.   • AgriJSON Schema can be accessed at https://agrijson.org
  • AgriJSON has been developed using JSON-LD standard.
  • The schema is useful to developers for designing interoperable systems in digital agriculture It is proposed that the AgriJSON Schema can be expanded in a phased manner to consist of about 100 dataObjects that across the entire spectrum of digital agriculture.

Agricultural Data: Critical for Sectoral Transformation

According to an estimate of NASSCOM, the annual economic potential of agricultural data is US$ 65 billion in India. The global projection may be much larger. The potential can be realized through multiple methods â€“ like digital services, analytics, AI-based predections, innovation and research. The economic potential of data lies along the entire value chain spanning across crop planning, crop management, logistics and marketing. The variety of agricultural data is wide as it deals with several complex aspects like land, soil, seed, crop, pest, disease, weather, market, machinery, devices, chemicals, quality, traceability and exports. Most often the data is required to be near real-time to be fit for purpose

Agriculture sector produces immense data. The emerging practices like smart farming, precision farming and climate smart agriculture are pushing up the volumes of data produced and consumed. IDC has assessed that currently in US, an average farm with a size of over 400 hectares, produces 0.5 million data points every day. This number is estimated to grow to 4 million by 2036. However, data can deliver on its promise of economic transformation only if it is of the right quality, standards-based, structured, and interoperable. Otherwise, data can become a burden instead of being an asset.

The Challenge

While the value of data is well recognized, the data ecosystem faces several challenges. The data is sioled. We do not have a data standard for agriculture like for healthcare which has FHIR (HL7) standard. This has led to the the challenge in exchange of the data. As a result, the potential of data lies untapped with its owners, while the innovators, researchers, and service providers grope or struggle for that data. Besides the difficulties in exchange of data, the non-standard representation of agricultural data makes It unsuitable for machine-to-machine interchange, readability, and interpretability. This is a huge barrier to designing, developing, and delivering efficient digital services to the stakeholers.

The Opportunity

  • An open standard for defining and representing agricultural data can unleash a huge potential currently latent in agricultural data at rest. The opportunity can be realized by pursuing multiple efforts of standardization along the data life cycle. These includes standardizing the vocabulary (already accomplished by the AGROVOC initiative of FAO), defining the semantics of agricultural data, developing a standard method for representation or serializtion of data, and the protocol for exchanging data. Significant opportunities lie in pursuing latter three ideas. Seamless interchange of agricultural data opens the following opportunities.
  • Mere digitization would enhance efficiencies of the agri value chain.
  • Emerging technologies enable data-driven, prediction-based farm decisions
  • Combinatorial power of data opens huge landscape of innovation
  • Smallholding farmers can get empowered through simple mobile interfaces
